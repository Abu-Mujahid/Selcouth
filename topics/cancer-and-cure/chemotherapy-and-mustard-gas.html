Chemotherapy & Mustard Gas

Chemotherapy & Mustard Gas.

https://www.cmaj.ca/content/189/8/E321.short

Summary from CMAJ

The Canadian Medical Association Journal (CMAJ) recounts how observations of mustard gas injuries in war revealed profound suppression of bone marrow and lymphoid tissue. Those findings directly informed the development of early alkylating agents, particularly nitrogen mustard, which produced the first documented remissions in certain lymphomas in the early 1940s. This historical thread links chemical warfare toxicology to the origins of modern chemotherapy, highlighting both the mechanistic rationale (rapidly dividing cells are vulnerable) and the severe toxicity that has always accompanied these drugs.
